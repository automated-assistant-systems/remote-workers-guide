# Editorial Style Guide
The Remote Worker’s Survival Guide in the AI Age  
Practical Tools, Workflows, and Strategies for Thriving Anywhere

Author: Gary G. Bayes

Purpose:
This document defines the writing and editing standards used throughout the manuscript.

It ensures consistency of:

- tone
- structure
- clarity
- examples
- workflow explanations

These guidelines apply to:

- human editors
- AI writing assistants
- manuscript reviewers

This document should be used alongside:

admin/book-structure-spec.md  
admin/manuscript-editor-brief.md

---

# 1. Audience

The book is written for:

- remote professionals
- freelancers
- consultants
- solopreneurs
- knowledge workers adapting to AI

Readers are intelligent professionals but **not necessarily technical experts**.

Writing should remain accessible without oversimplifying ideas.

Preferred reading level:

Grade 8–10.

---

# 2. Core Tone

The voice of the book should remain:

- practical
- clear
- calm
- encouraging
- realistic
- professional but approachable

The tone should feel like **an experienced professional explaining practical workflows to a capable colleague**.

Avoid hype, exaggerated predictions, or overly technical explanations.

---

# 3. Tone to Avoid

Avoid writing that sounds:

Overly promotional

Example to avoid:

AI will revolutionize everything and eliminate most work.

Alarmist

Workers who fail to adopt AI will be left behind forever.

Overly technical

The transformer architecture leverages multi-head attention to produce latent representations.

Prefer balanced explanations.

Example:

AI tools can accelerate repetitive tasks, but they still require human judgment.

---

# 4. Writing Principles

## Prefer Clarity Over Complexity

Use simple, direct language.

Avoid unnecessarily complex phrasing.

Example:

Weak

The implementation of AI-enabled workflow frameworks facilitates productivity transformation.

Better

AI tools can help streamline repetitive tasks and simplify remote workflows.

---

## Avoid Generic AI Language

AI-generated writing often contains predictable filler phrases.

Avoid phrases like:

- in today’s rapidly evolving world
- leverage cutting-edge technology
- revolutionary breakthrough
- game-changing innovation
- it is important to note that

These add little meaning.

Replace them with specific explanations.

---

## Prefer Concrete Examples

Abstract statements are less helpful than real workflows.

Weak

AI improves productivity.

Better

AI can summarize meetings, draft emails, and organize research notes.

Whenever possible, explain **how work actually changes**.

---

## Maintain a Human Perspective

The book should never sound like an AI system speaking.

Avoid:

As an AI system, I recommend…

Prefer:

Many professionals find that…

---

## Respect the Reader’s Intelligence

Avoid patronizing explanations.

Explain ideas clearly while assuming readers are capable professionals.

---

# 5. Paragraph Style

Paragraphs should remain short and focused.

Typical paragraph length:

2–4 sentences.

Each paragraph should communicate **one main idea**.

Large blocks of text should be split.

---

# 6. Section Structure

Most sections should follow this structure:

1. Concept explanation  
2. Why the idea matters  
3. Example or case study  
4. Practical workflow  
5. Tools that support the workflow  
6. Key takeaway

This structure helps readers move from **understanding to action**.

---

# 7. Chapter Structure

Chapters should generally follow this pattern.

Opening Hook

Concept Explanation

Visual Explanation

Example or Case Study

Key Insight

Chapter Takeaways

Action Plan

Transition to the Next Chapter

Not every chapter must contain every section, but the structure should remain recognizable.

---

# 8. Case Study Standards

Examples in the book must be:

- fictional
- realistic
- clearly labeled

Use the following label:

*Fictional example based on common remote work situations.*

Examples should reflect realistic scenarios involving:

- freelancers
- consultants
- distributed teams
- remote professionals

Avoid unrealistic outcomes such as:

- instant success
- dramatic income increases
- unrealistic productivity gains

Improvements should feel **incremental and practical**.

---

# 9. Editing Guidelines

Editors should improve:

- clarity
- readability
- flow
- logical transitions

while preserving the author’s voice.

Editing should make the writing **clearer and stronger**, not fundamentally different.

---

## Reduce Wordiness

Prefer concise sentences.

Before

At this point in time many professionals are beginning to recognize the potential benefits of AI tools.

After

Many professionals are beginning to recognize the benefits of AI tools.

---

## Remove Repetition

Avoid repeating the same ideas across chapters.

Common repetition to reduce:

- explanations of what AI is
- general discussions about the future of work
- repeated tool descriptions

If a concept was explained earlier, reference it instead of repeating it.

---

## Strengthen Transitions

Sections should flow logically.

Weak

AI tools are powerful. Remote work has also grown.

Better

As remote work has expanded, AI tools have become an important way to manage distributed workflows.

---

## Flag Weak Logic

Editors should identify:

- unsupported claims
- unclear reasoning
- exaggerated conclusions

These should be flagged for fact-checking.

---

# 10. Fact and Claim Standards

Never invent:

- statistics
- research studies
- citations
- product capabilities

If a claim cannot be verified:

- remove it
- soften it
- or flag it for review.

Accuracy is more important than sounding authoritative.

---

# 11. AI Tool References

When referencing tools:

Use tools as examples rather than prescriptions.

Example:

Tools such as ChatGPT, Notion AI, or similar assistants can summarize meeting notes.

Avoid:

You should always use ChatGPT.

The goal is to emphasize **workflows rather than specific products**.

---

# 12. Prompt Examples

Prompt examples should remain:

- short
- clear
- copyable

Prompts should include:

Purpose  
Prompt text  
Optional instructions  
Reminder to review AI output

Example:

Prompt:

Summarize the following meeting transcript and list the key action items.

Review reminder:

Verify important details before sharing the summary.

---

# 13. Technical Depth

Readers are professionals but not AI engineers.

Avoid deep technical explanations of:

- neural networks
- transformer architectures
- model training mechanics

Focus instead on:

- practical usage
- workflows
- decision-making

---

# 14. Redundancy Control

If similar explanations appear across chapters:

Keep the clearest explanation in the **original chapter**.

Later chapters should reference it.

Example:

As discussed in Chapter 6, effective prompts provide context and clear instructions.

This reduces unnecessary repetition.

---

# 15. Voice Consistency

The book should consistently sound like:

an experienced professional explaining practical workflows developed through real-world experimentation.

Avoid:

- academic tone
- corporate marketing language
- exaggerated productivity claims

Preferred tone:

clear explanations  
practical advice  
balanced perspective

---

# 16. Final Editorial Checklist

Before finalizing a chapter verify:

- Tone remains calm and practical
- Sentences are clear and concise
- Paragraphs are short and focused
- Examples are clearly labeled fictional
- Workflows are explained step-by-step
- Prompts remain copyable
- Repetition has been reduced
- Transitions connect sections logically
