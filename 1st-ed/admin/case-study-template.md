# Case Study Template

*The Remote Worker’s Survival Guide in the AI Age*

All case studies in this book are **fictional but based on common real-world scenarios**.

They should feel realistic, practical, and relatable to remote professionals.

---

# Case Study: [Short Descriptive Title]

Example: *The Overloaded Consultant*

---

## Background

Introduce the professional.

Include:

* profession or industry
* remote work context
* typical responsibilities

Example:

Alex is a freelance designer who works remotely with clients in several different time zones. His work involves creative design, but a significant portion of his week is spent responding to emails, organizing feedback, and preparing project updates.

---

## The Challenge

Describe the problem the person is facing.

Examples:

* excessive administrative work
* scattered information across tools
* difficulty managing multiple clients
* time spent writing repetitive communications

The challenge should feel realistic and relatable.

---

## The Change

Explain how the professional introduced an AI-supported workflow.

This might include:

* using AI to summarize meetings
* generating drafts of routine messages
* organizing research notes
* automating repetitive documentation

Keep the explanation simple.

---

## The Workflow

Provide a clear sequence of actions.

Example:

1. Meeting transcripts were captured automatically.
2. An AI assistant generated a meeting summary.
3. Action items were extracted into a task manager.
4. A draft update email was created for the client.

This section should demonstrate how AI supports the workflow rather than replaces the person.

---

## The Result

Describe the outcome.

Possible results include:

* reduced administrative workload
* improved client communication
* faster research or writing
* better organization

Example:

By introducing this workflow, Alex reduced the time spent on administrative work by nearly half and was able to take on an additional client without increasing his working hours.

---

## Lesson Learned

End with the broader takeaway.

Example:

Small workflow improvements can produce large productivity gains. AI works best when it assists with repetitive tasks while professionals focus on creative and strategic work.

---

## Writing Guidelines for Case Studies

Case studies should:

* remain concise
* avoid exaggerated claims
* focus on realistic productivity improvements
* illustrate practical workflows

Avoid unrealistic outcomes such as:

* instant business success
* eliminating all work
* AI replacing human expertise entirely.
