# AI Writing Rules

*The Remote Worker’s Survival Guide in the AI Age*

These rules guide how AI systems assist with drafting content for this book.
Their purpose is to maintain **clarity, credibility, and a consistent author voice** while avoiding the common weaknesses of AI-generated writing.

---

# Core Principle

AI should act as a **supporting writing assistant**, not the author.

The final voice of the book must remain:

* practical
* professional
* calm
* realistic
* helpful

Avoid writing that sounds promotional, exaggerated, or overly technical.

---

# Rule 1 — Prefer Clarity Over Complexity

AI often produces unnecessarily complex sentences.

Prefer simple, clear language.

**Avoid**

> The implementation of AI-enabled workflow frameworks facilitates a transformative productivity paradigm.

**Prefer**

> AI tools can help streamline repetitive tasks and make remote workflows easier to manage.

Shorter sentences are almost always better.

---

# Rule 2 — Avoid Generic AI Language

AI models frequently use predictable filler phrases.

Avoid phrases like:

* "In today’s rapidly evolving world"
* "It is important to note that"
* "Leverage cutting-edge technology"
* "Revolutionary breakthrough"
* "Game-changing innovation"

These phrases add little meaning.

Replace them with **specific explanations or examples**.

---

# Rule 3 — Use Concrete Examples

Abstract explanations are less helpful than practical ones.

**Weak**

> AI improves productivity.

**Better**

> AI can summarize meeting transcripts, generate draft emails, and organize research notes.

Always prefer specific workflows over general statements.

---

# Rule 4 — Do Not Invent Facts

AI systems sometimes generate plausible but incorrect information.

Never invent:

* statistics
* studies
* citations
* product capabilities
* research findings

If a fact cannot be verified, it must be presented as:

* a possibility
* an observation
* or removed entirely.

---

# Rule 5 — Avoid Exaggeration

This book emphasizes realistic productivity improvements.

Avoid exaggerated claims such as:

* "AI will transform every job."
* "AI eliminates most work."
* "AI guarantees productivity."

Prefer balanced wording.

Example:

> AI can significantly reduce time spent on repetitive tasks when used effectively.

---

# Rule 6 — Maintain a Human Perspective

AI should never present itself as the authority.

The book speaks from the perspective of an experienced professional guiding readers through a changing work environment.

Avoid language such as:

> As an AI system, I recommend...

Instead write:

> Many professionals find that...

---

# Rule 7 — Keep Paragraphs Focused

Each paragraph should communicate **one idea**.

AI tends to create long paragraphs with multiple concepts.

When editing:

* split large paragraphs
* clarify the main point
* remove unnecessary sentences

---

# Rule 8 — Avoid Repetition Across Chapters

Common topics appear frequently in AI discussions.

Avoid repeating explanations such as:

* what AI is
* basic definitions of automation
* general discussions about the future of work

If the concept was explained earlier, refer back to it rather than repeating it fully.

---

# Rule 9 — Maintain Realistic Case Studies

Case studies must feel plausible.

Avoid unrealistic scenarios such as:

* instant success
* dramatic income increases
* unrealistic productivity gains

Most improvements should be **incremental and practical**.

---

# Rule 10 — Use Structured Sections

When drafting chapters, prefer consistent structure.

Typical flow:

1. Concept explanation
2. Why the idea matters
3. Case study example
4. Practical workflow
5. Tools that support the workflow
6. Key takeaway

Structure improves readability and learning.

---

# Rule 11 — Avoid Overly Technical Explanations

Readers of this book are professionals, not AI researchers.

Avoid detailed explanations of:

* machine learning architectures
* transformer models
* neural network mechanics

Focus on **how tools are used**, not how they are built.

---

# Rule 12 — Prefer Practical Advice

The book should feel actionable.

Whenever possible include:

* workflows
* checklists
* step-by-step processes
* examples from real work situations

Readers should be able to apply the ideas immediately.

---

# Rule 13 — Maintain Consistent Tone

The book should always sound:

* calm
* informed
* supportive
* pragmatic

Avoid dramatic language such as:

* "the end of work"
* "the AI revolution will change everything"

Balanced perspective builds trust.

---

# Rule 14 — Identify Uncertainty

If the future impact of AI is unclear, say so.

Example:

> It is still too early to know how widely these tools will be adopted, but early adopters are already experimenting with them.

Honesty increases credibility.

---

# Rule 15 — Respect the Reader’s Intelligence

Readers should never feel that the text is oversimplified or patronizing.

The goal is to explain complex ideas clearly while respecting the reader’s experience.

---

# Final Guideline

The best AI-assisted writing for this book should feel like it was written by a knowledgeable professional who has spent years working remotely and experimenting with productivity tools.

The goal is **practical insight, not technological hype**.
